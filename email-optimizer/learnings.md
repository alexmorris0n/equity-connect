# Consolidated Learnings

This file contains all validated insights from email copy experiments. Updated after each harvest.

---

## Known Facts (Pre-Experiment)

- 3,242 emails sent with current copy → 0% real human reply rate
- All 19 "replies" were inbound spam bots (verified by tracking codewords)
- Bounce rate is healthy at 2.7% (under 5% threshold)
- Lead data quality is good -- real emails, real people, legitimate consumer domains
- Sending infrastructure is healthy -- all accounts warmed 5+ months, scores 93-100
- The problem is channel-audience fit AND copy, not infrastructure

## What We Know Doesn't Work

- Long subject lines that signal marketing intent
- Explaining reverse mortgages upfront in email #1
- Burying the calculator link in email #3
- 4-email sequences that over-explain the product
- Opening with self-introduction ("I'm X with Equity Connect")

## What We Think Might Work (Untested)

- Ultra-short emails (under 50 words)
- Vague/curiosity subject lines
- Leading with the calculator link
- Removing all product mentions from email #1
- Questions instead of statements
- First-name-only subject lines
